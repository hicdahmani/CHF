Call for Papers & Submissions

We welcome diverse forms of scholarly and creative contribution

Submission Guidelines

Submission Deadline

February 28, 2024

Notification Date

April 15, 2024

Conference Dates

June 10-12, 2024

Submission Types

We invite submissions in the following formats:

Research Papers

Full research papers (5,000-8,000 words) presenting original scholarly work that engages with one or more conference themes.

Format: Anonymized PDF with academic references

Position Papers

Conceptual or argumentative papers (3,000-5,000 words) presenting a position, debate, or critical perspective on key conference questions.

Format: Anonymized PDF with references

Panel Proposals

Proposed panels (4-5 presentations) exploring a theme or question in depth. Submit a panel abstract (500 words) plus brief bios of panelists.

Format: PDF with speaker information

Creative Works

Artistic, literary, or multimedia works that engage with conference themes. Include a 500-word artist/author statement explaining the work's engagement with our questions.

Format: Varies (PDFs, links, etc.)

Practitioner Cases

Case studies or reflective accounts (3,000-5,000 words) from educators, designers, policymakers, or practitioners implementing or thinking about intelligent systems in practice.

Format: PDF with any relevant appendices

Workshop Proposals

Interactive workshops (2-3 hours) that invite participants into dialogue, experimentation, or co-creation. Submit a proposal (500 words) with learning objectives and format.

Format: PDF with detailed description

Selection Criteria

Submissions will be evaluated based on:

  • Relevance: Clear engagement with one or more conference themes and guiding questions
  • Quality: Strong conceptual thinking, clear expression, and appropriate methodology
  • Originality: Novel perspectives, approaches, or insights
  • Inclusivity: Representation of diverse voices, epistemologies, and ways of knowing
  • Impact: Potential to advance dialogue and contribute to the field
  • Accessibility: Clear communication that engages multiple disciplines and backgrounds

Important Notes

  • All submissions should be original work not previously published
  • Submitted papers should be anonymized for blind review
  • Authors should avoid self-identifying information in the submission
  • We are committed to a diverse, inclusive conference program
  • Accepted authors will be required to register for the conference
  • Selected papers may be considered for publication in a dedicated volume
